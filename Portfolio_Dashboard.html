Key Findings & Benchmark Deltas

  • Multi-Factor Alpha: The portfolio successfully blends Quality (SPHQ), High Dividend (VHYD/VYMI), and broad Market-Cap weighting (MGC), creating a smoother volatility profile than a pure SPY holding.
  • Geographic Tilt: Holds ~30% International exposure (vs. 0% in SPY/QQQ), providing a strong hedge against US market mean reversion. Ex-China EM (EMXC) avoids specific geopolitical drag.
  • Value/Yield Bias: Due to VHYD and VYMI, the portfolio has a higher yield (~2.34%) and heavier Financials/Energy weight than QQQ or SPY.

Actionable Recommendations

  • 1. Address the Cap-Size Gap: The portfolio is overwhelmingly Large/Mega-cap. Consider replacing 5-10% of MGC with a Small-Cap Value ETF (e.g., AVUV or IJR) to capture small-size premiums.
  • 2. Monitor Tech Overlap: MGC, SPHQ, and AOR all carry heavy AAPL, MSFT, and NVDA. While total weight is lower than QQQ, tracking these specific names is vital.
  • 3. Fixed Income Optimization: The 6% Fixed Income (from AOR) is too small to act as a definitive equity crash hedge. Consider either eliminating AOR for pure equity or increasing dedicated treasuries (e.g., TLT) if capital preservation is needed.
